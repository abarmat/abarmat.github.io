Think harder

Aug 14, 2025

The mind is everything. What you think you become. — Buddha

I've been thinking a lot about how we use our mind these days. Endless scrolling on infinite feeds and screaming notifications. We are stuck in the habit of devouring novelty like it's our last meal. When we're stuck in this passive loop, we're barely scratching the surface of our minds, we miss out on the deeper layers. We don't fully navigate the latent space, and that's where the real magic happens.

The latent space is a concept borrowed from AI. Learning is the process by which we create a compressed representation of the world, where data gets distilled into patterns and connections.

===

We live under a constant shortening of our attention span. I don't really know how it work, but making a parallel with artificial neural network, we are rewarding our attention mechanism too soon, we clear our context window and move to the next thing. We can't sustain focus anymore. Why would we? The next dopamine hit is just a scroll away. Even if you are conscious about that, it's really hard to break the loop.

Constant passive consumption means we are destroying our agency. Again, we get rewards in the form of novelty just for being there. We don't need to do any action.

In 2007 I founded a company to do software for mobile. Mobile was the cool new thing in town. The iPhone was not even still around, and very few people carried one. Their sole purpose was calls and text messaging, and there was no wifi. We just had very early versions with color screens that could run a few very basic apps.

While taking the subway in Buenos Aires, as I watched people lost in their inner thoughts, in a few years all these people will be staring at a screen. Immediately after, I wondered about new mental health issues might emerge. We are going to consume an astounding amount of information, how will our brain cope with that? My intuition was right, but I could not imagine how it would turn. It seems that we affected our rewards centers, and with it, our attention.
