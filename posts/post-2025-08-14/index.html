Think harder

The mind is everything. What you think you become. — Buddha

I've been thinking a lot about how we use our mind these days. Endless scrolling on infinite feeds and screaming notifications. We are stuck in the habit of devouring novelty like it's our last meal. When we're stuck in this passive loop, we're barely scratching the surface of our minds, we miss out on the deeper layers. We don't fully navigate the latent space, and that's where the real magic happens.

The latent space is a concept borrowed from AI. Learning is the process by which we create a compressed representation of the world, where data gets distilled into patterns and connections.

===

Our minds work similarly: experiences, thoughts, and memories form this vast representation of reality. But constant input from screens floods it with noise. Inference, memory formation and learning, it all happens as side effects of neural activations.

Zombie scrolling is relentless. It’s not about fear; it’s about the itch for more. A new meme, a hot take, a random fact—each hit feeds the craving, pinging our dopamine like a slot machine. I’ve been there, scrolling for hours, chasing novelty but feeling emptier with every swipe. It’s not building anything; it’s just skimming the surface of what our minds can do.

You're not building anything; you're just reacting, trapped in a cycle that drains your mental energy. I've felt it myself—hours lost to outrage, emerging more anxious than enlightened.

But reflection? That's the game-changer. It's like fine-tuning your model. You pause, sift through the clutter, and pick out the hidden gems—those insights or connections that spark something new. Feed them back, and you create fresh activations, strengthening pathways, uncovering deeper truths. Whether it's journaling after a long day or meditating on a walk, reflection lets you navigate your mind's latent space fully. It's not just about consuming; it's about creating.

In my experience, stepping away from the scroll energizes you. You reclaim control, turn noise into wisdom. Next time you're tempted to doomscroll, try this: close the app, grab a notebook, and reflect. What activations stood out today? Dive in.
